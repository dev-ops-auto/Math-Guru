🏆 Quick Tips Spotlight
01
Multiply by 11 instantly

For a 2-digit number AB: Answer = A, (A+B), B. Example: 43 × 11 = 4, (4+3), 3 = 473

02
Squares ending in 5

Multiply the tens digit by (tens+1), then append 25. Example: 65² = 6×7 followed by 25 = 4225

03
Divisibility by 9

Sum all digits. If divisible by 9, the number is too. Example: 729 → 7+2+9=18 → 18÷9=2 ✓

💡 Maths Tips & Tricks – All Classes
01
Multiply any number by 11

For two-digit AB: put A first, then A+B in middle, then B. If middle sum ≥10, carry over. 73×11 = 7, 10, 3 → carry: 803

02
Squaring numbers ending in 5

Multiply tens digit by (tens+1), append 25. 85² → 8×9=72 → 7225

03
Multiply by 9 quickly

Multiply by 10, subtract the number. 37×9 = 370−37 = 333

04
Multiply by 99

Multiply by 100, subtract the number. 47×99 = 4700−47 = 4653

05
Divisibility by 3

Sum of digits divisible by 3 → number is divisible. 123 → 1+2+3=6 ✓

06
Divisibility by 4

If last two digits form a number divisible by 4. 2348 → 48÷4=12 ✓

07
Divisibility by 6

Divisible by both 2 and 3. 126 → Even ✓, 1+2+6=9 ÷3 ✓

08
Divisibility by 8

Last three digits divisible by 8. 5120 → 120÷8=15 ✓

09
Finding square root fast

Group digits in pairs from right. Estimate for each pair. √1764 → √(17|64) → 42

10
Vedic Maths – Base method

For numbers near 100: e.g. 97×98 = (97−2)×100 + (3×2) = 9506

11
Remember trig values

sin 0°=0, 30°=½, 45°=1/√2, 60°=√3/2, 90°=1. Pattern: √0/2, √1/2, √2/2, √3/2, √4/2.

12
LCM × HCF = Product

For two numbers a,b: LCM(a,b) × HCF(a,b) = a × b

13
Sum of first n natural numbers

1+2+3+...+n = n(n+1)/2. For n=100: 100×101/2 = 5050

14
Sum of squares

1²+2²+...+n² = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6

15
Sum of cubes

1³+2³+...+n³ = [n(n+1)/2]²

16
Percentage shortcut

x% of y = y% of x. So 8% of 25 = 25% of 8 = 2 (easier!)

17
Multiply two numbers near a base

Both near 50: use (50+a)(50+b) = 2500 + 50(a+b) + ab

18
Factoring difference of squares

a²−b² = (a+b)(a−b). e.g. 91×89 = 90²−1 = 8099

19
Butterfly method for fractions

a/b + c/d = (ad+bc)/(bd). Cross multiply numerators, add, keep product as denominator.

20
Quick cube roots

Memorize last digits: cube of 1→1, 2→8, 3→7, 4→4, 5→5, 6→6, 7→3, 8→2, 9→9, 0→0.
